About SIDBI

India’s apex institution for MSME development

Small Industries Development Bank of India was established in 1990 by an Act of Parliament as the principal financial institution for the promotion, financing and development of India's MSME sector. SIDBI supports MSMEs through finance, ecosystem development and technology-driven lending solutions.

About Regional Rural Bank

Driving financial inclusion across rural India

Regional Rural Banks were established under the Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976 to bring institutional credit to rural India. Since their inception in 1975, RRBs have played a crucial role in supporting agriculture, small businesses, and rural entrepreneurship.

The power of SIDBI × RRB collaboration

The SIDBI–RRB Co-Lending Arrangement combines the strengths of both institutions to expand access to MSME financing.

The framework behind the Co-Lending Model

Under the Co-Lending Arrangement framework introduced by the Reserve Bank of India, regulated financial institutions can jointly fund loans while sharing risk and returns.

1

RRBs originate MSME loans

RRBs serve as the originating institution and take responsibility for customer onboarding.

2

SIDBI co-funds the portfolio

They provide frameworks and digital systems that help RRBs originate and manage MSME loans.

3

Sharing revenue and risks

The model creates a synergistic partnership that leverages both institutions to support MSMEs.
